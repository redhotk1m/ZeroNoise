About us

Who are we?

We are a team of four students, who’s goal is to make everyone’s computer experience better. The computer Is a great tool, and should be accessible for everyone, no matter what kind of disability one might have.

Epilepsy

It’s possible for everyone to get a seizure under certain conditions, however, people with diagnosed epilepsy has a greater chance than others. Seizures are often accompanied with cramps, but this only applies to 30% of those who are diagnosed with Epilepsy. Some epileptics live perfectly normal lives, while others experience great challenges. In Norway alone, there are about 45 000 people with diagnosed epilepsy. ZeroNoise is made to help people combat these challenges.

Sound sensitivity

Being sensitive to sound may not be as known as epilepsy, but it’s not as uncommon as you might believe. However, not only those with sound sensitivity, may experience problems. There are several videos online that aren’t sound normalized, and might blow your eardrums out. If you’ve ever watched a homemade Youtube video, and read the comments like “RIP headphone users”, you know what we’re talking about. ZeroNoise is here to fix that!

Easy to use

Both this website and ZeroNoise is made to be easy and accessible. ZeroNoise will give you many presets you can choose upon first run/install, with clear instructions on how to use the program. You can also turn off “run on startup”, as this might not be wanted by everyone. If you’re more computer oriented you can go to “advanced” settings to customize it perfectly to your needs.

We realize that this program isn’t what most people would want to install, and it might be hard to find for those who need it. Therefore, we’re teaming up with Windows, so that it gets automatically installed by Windows update. For those who are using old computers, with old operative systems, you can download it manually from this website. That is why we’ve made the website accessible for everyone, no matter how computer experienced you might be, or if you have bad eyesight/blindness/deafness etc.

We’ve used few colors, with strong contrasts, to accommodate colorblind people. We’ve also used a known font, 60 letters per line on computers, and 40 letters per line on phones, to make it easier to read. This website is compatible with every browser, and even IE8. We’ve avoided using any new code, and only used JavaScript when we had no other option. Even if JavaScript doesn’t work, it would have little to no impact on the website at all.

Every link has underline, and navigation in general is easy. To accommodate blind users, we’ve used nav, strong, non-repeating alts etc. We’ve also only used relative units like “Ems” and % for those who needs to zoom a lot. The website works great on all phones and all computers, with no loss of functionality.
